In “Society”

  • Supporting the Music and Video Industry
  • Supporting Educational and Artistic Activities
  • Provision of Opportunities to Work

With the unprecidented changes society in general has experienced recently, the importance of music and video in everyday lives has become even more paramount. They help to build communities through shared passions and values, and during these times of fragmented contact, people are actively seeking to develop connections to enhance their lives.

At Roland - with our mix of products, services, and corporate culture - we are uniquely placed to support the development of arts, and we continue to strive for any contribution we can make, both in music and video and society at large.

Supporting the Music and Video Industry

Due to worldwide spread of COVID-19, music events and activities have been greatly impacted, not only for professional artists, but also crew, general players and performers.

Now, more than ever, it is important to provide opportunities for our community to engage together. To support this, we are running a global program of activities designed to inspire and support the music community. Below are just a few of the highlights.

Provision of Platforms for Activities

To support artists in Japan, over the past two years we have hosted an exciting online festival called ‘Players Summit’.

Aimed at both musicians and the general public, the theme for 2021 was ‘Let's be connected with music’. Over the three-day festival, we produced a series of content including; live music, seminars on composing music, and an amazing open-mic session featuring one hundred amateur guests.

We are also continuing with ‘PROJECT MyRoland’, which provides customers with opportunities and content to make the most of their Roland products.

PROJECT MyRoland consists of many events, including; social media performances, gatherings, product development discussion meetings, get-togethers of users, and performance workshops. Such engagement not only provides the opportunity for people to enjoy music togther, but also enables Roland to better understand the needs of our customers when developing future products or services.

Roland has developed a wide range of Audio/Video products for both professional-use and more casual users looking to develop and stream content using their smartphones. While the COVID-19 pandemic poses some restrictions on the activities of many artists and creators, if they do choose new ways to engage with their fans online, our line of products can strongly support them. From single camera setups to full multi-media switches, Roland truly has a solution for all content creators.

In the U.S., Verzuz TV (verzuztv.com) is an award-winning webcast series created by producers Timbaland and Swizz Beatz which streams live DJ battles between big-name hip-hop artists. Born during the pandemic, Verzuz needed a partner to enable high-quality sound and video streaming - Roland naturally stepped up to the plate and has now become synonymous not only the quality of our instruments, but also how to deliver that experience around the world online.

For musical and video culture to develop, it is also necessary to have an environment where we can share and discuss values to support each other, while respecting the diversity of the people taking action. We will therefore continue to provide a platform to nurture conversations to help tackle inequalities around the world.

Supporting Activities of Artists and the Industry

In order to support the Japanese live music entertainment industry, we have raised a supporting fund through the cloud funding scheme, and made a donation to “Music Cross Aid” (the fund for supporting live entertainment businesses). We will continue committing ourselves in supporting the entire music industry.

Among our similar activities, we have sponsored the launch of a free streaming network entitled “We Are Hear: On The Air” to support donations to the “MusiCares® COVID-19 Relief Fund” designed for artists and those working in the industry, in cooperation with the Los Angeles-based artist empowerment label/management/publishing company “We Are Hear”. Roland is supporting their activities multidirectionally through the supply of professional Audio/Video live-streaming production resources, including; switchers, cameras, and manpower.

Supporting Educational and Artistic Activities

Support of Educational Institutions

We are providing educational institutions offering musical production courses, such as “Point Blank Music School” in the U.K. and “JMC Academy” of Australia, with high-quality software synthesizers and music production software, via our Roland Cloud service.

In addition, we are donating products and making contributions to educational institutions in various countries. We support “Resonance Music College” in the U.K. by providing equipment and holding seminars, etc. And, in Canada, we have been supporting for many years “Music for Young Children” to develop childhood sociability and cognition through music.

We are commited to contribute to the development of next-generation artists and to the benefits of music in mind development - thereby helping to protect music culture and to build a more educated and affluent society.

Support for Disabled People's Artistic Activities

As part of our activities to more widely support arts and culture, we are backing the “Paralym Art” project in Japan run by the general incorporated association for supporting independence of people with disabilities (Shogaisha Jiritu Suishin Kiko). At the same time, we continue to contribute to the economic independence of disabled artists and creation of ties within the society, we are making good use of their artistic works in our in-house publications, etc.

Cooperation and Support of Charitable Organizations, etc.

We are donating musical instruments to U.K.-based charity “Musica.” Roland supports their activities aimed at increasing self-esteem and happiness of underprivileged people owing to disability, poverty or isolation, etc. through creative activities.

We are also supporting “Ronald McDonald House” to help children fighting against serious diseases, and cooperate with such music therapy organizations “Nordoff-Robbins” and the “Music Therapy association of British Columbia”. We will continue contributing to the growth of society where the power of music can help improve everyone's life.

Provision of Opportunities to Work

Our Engagement for People with Disabilities

At Roland Corporation, we place emphasis on improvement of the work environment so that people with disabilities can maximise their capabilities and opportunities, become independent, and work with a sense of security. We do not limit nor discrimate against any disability, instead we work with staff to ensure given tasks are within agreed physical and mental abilities. As with all staff, we then enter a programme of continuous development to fully realise each person's potential.

In addition, we aim to eradicate or mitigate concerns about health or work, by providing an industry leading support structure. This includes reguar access to psychologists, psychiatrists and counseling. We continue to build workplaces with high accessibility, including priority parking, and user-friendly facilities.

Furthermore, we also fully cooperate with Roland locations around the world, other companies, local governments, welfare organizations, and the community in general to ensure our standards set the best example to society in how to provide independence and security to disabled employees.

Activity to Deliver Education to Students

As part of our commitment to the development of society, Roland Corporation run a comprehensive programme of internships and explanatory meetings on the company for students. We feel that by providing positive opportunities and experiences to students, we will fuel development of those individuals and society in general, as well as nuturing talent for the future of Roland.

Internship course

We carry out two student internship periods, one for summer and the other during winter. Through the experience of planning products, students can learn the fundamentals of business, problem-solving techniques, marketing activity, presentation skills and more. We have continuously accepted interns so that we will be able to contribute to the increase of basic social knowledge, development of the sense of professionalism and arousal of motivation for learning.

Explanatory meetings on the company for junior and senior high-school students

To provide the junior and senior high-school students with opportunities to think about their own vocational aptitude or their future designs, we are holding explanatory meetings on the company in cooperation with local communities.

In 2021, we participated in one of the Hamamatsu City's gender-equality projects designed for female junior and senior high-school students entitled “Go in the engineering course 2021: Tour of the actual scenes of work”. The aim was to promote further education or work within engineering fields through first-hand experience within companies. We are planning to continue positively participating in similar explanatory meetings in the future.
